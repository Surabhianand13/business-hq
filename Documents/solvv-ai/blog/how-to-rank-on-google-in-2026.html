in 2026: The Only SEO

Step 1: Get Your Technical SEO Right

Before you write a single piece of content, your technical foundation must be solid. Google can't rank a page it can't properly crawl and index.

Technical SEO checklist for 2026:

  • Unique, keyword-rich title tag on every page (under 60 characters)
  • Compelling meta description on every page (under 155 characters)
  • Canonical tags to avoid duplicate content issues
  • XML sitemap submitted to Google Search Console
  • robots.txt configured correctly
  • HTTPS across the entire site (not just the homepage)
  • Structured data (JSON-LD) for your business type, articles, FAQs
  • Core Web Vitals passing: LCP < 2.5s, CLS < 0.1, FID/INP < 200ms
  • No broken links (run a crawl with Screaming Frog monthly)

Step 2: Build E-E-A-T Signals

Google's Quality Rater Guidelines now place enormous weight on Experience, Expertise, Authoritativeness, and Trust. For small businesses, this means:

  • Experience: Show real work, real clients, real results. Portfolio pages with before/after. Case studies with numbers.
  • Expertise: Author bios on every article. Credentials displayed prominently. Content written with genuine depth, not surface-level summaries.
  • Authoritativeness: Get mentioned on other reputable websites. Trade publications, industry directories, press coverage.
  • Trust: Physical address (even if a registered address), phone number, SSL, clear privacy policy, terms, and real testimonials with full names.
💡

Key insight for 2026: Google now cross-references your website content with what people say about your business across the web. Consistency between your claims and third-party validation (reviews, mentions) is increasingly important for Trust signals.

Step 3: Create Content That Ranks (The Right Way)

Content is still king — but the type of content that ranks has changed dramatically. Here's what works in 2026:

Target Specific, Longer Keywords

Short keywords ("web design") are dominated by giant sites with domain authority you can't match. Long-tail keywords ("AI web design agency for e-commerce startups") have lower competition and higher commercial intent. Start there.

Write for Search Intent, Not Keyword Density

Google's AI understands what a searcher actually wants. Someone searching "how to rank on Google" wants a guide, not a services page. Match your content format to the intent: informational queries get guides, transactional queries get product/service pages.

Go Deep, Not Wide

One comprehensive, 2,000-word guide on a specific topic outperforms ten 300-word shallow posts. The Helpful Content system penalises thin content that exists to rank rather than to genuinely help the reader.

Update Content Regularly

A post published in 2023 that hasn't been touched since is stale. Google rewards freshness, especially for topics that evolve (like SEO itself). Audit your content every 6 months and update outdated statistics, examples, and recommendations.

Step 4: Optimise for AI Search (AEO)

In 2026, ranking on Google isn't the only goal. Answer Engine Optimisation (AEO) means getting your content cited in AI Overviews, ChatGPT responses, Perplexity answers, and other AI-generated summaries. This is a completely new traffic channel.

How to get cited by AI engines:

  • Write clear, direct answers to specific questions (AI models love quotable paragraphs)
  • Use structured headings (H2, H3) that match question formats
  • Add FAQ schema markup to your pages
  • Build topical authority — cover a subject comprehensively across multiple pages
  • Get cited on authoritative third-party sites (Wikipedia, major publications, industry databases)

Step 5: Build Real Backlinks

Backlinks remain a top-3 ranking factor in 2026. But spammy link building actively hurts you now. Focus on genuine authority links:

  • Digital PR: Get quoted in industry articles, offer expert commentary to journalists via HARO (Help a Reporter Out)
  • Guest posting: Write genuinely useful articles for respected blogs in your niche — not link farm directories
  • Business directories: Clutch, G2, Crunchbase, industry-specific directories — these are high-authority, relevant links
  • Client mentions: Ask clients to mention or link to you in their "team" or "partners" pages
  • Create linkable assets: Original research, unique data, free tools, or comprehensive guides that people naturally want to link to

Step 6: Don't Ignore Local SEO (Even for Global Businesses)

Google Business Profile is free and extremely powerful. Even if you serve clients globally, having a complete, verified Google Business Profile improves your credibility signals and helps you appear in local searches from your home market. Complete every field, add photos, and collect reviews consistently.
