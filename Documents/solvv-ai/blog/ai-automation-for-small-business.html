What Should You Automate First?

Not everything is worth automating. Focus on tasks that are:

  • Repetitive: If you do the same thing more than 3 times per week, it's a candidate
  • Rule-based: "If X happens, do Y" — these are perfect for automation
  • High volume: The more times a task runs, the bigger the payback
  • Low-stakes for errors: Don't automate anything where a mistake would cause serious harm without human review

The Best Automation Tools for SMBs in 2026

You don't need to code to automate. These no-code and low-code tools connect your apps together:

  • n8n — open-source, self-hostable, extremely powerful. Steep learning curve but zero subscription cost at scale.
  • Make (formerly Integromat) — visual workflow builder, excellent for complex multi-step automations. More powerful than Zapier for the same cost.
  • Zapier — easiest to use, widest app library (6,000+ integrations). Best for simple, one-step automations. Expensive at scale.
  • ActivePieces — newer open-source alternative to Zapier. Growing fast with strong AI integration support.

How to Start: The 3-Step Process

  1. Audit your time: For one week, log every task you do that feels repetitive. Note how long it takes and how often you do it.
  2. Pick your top 3: Choose the 3 tasks that take the most time and follow a clear rule ("if form submitted, add to CRM"). These are your first automations.
  3. Build one at a time: Don't try to automate everything at once. Build one workflow, run it for 2 weeks, refine it, then add the next. Solid beats fast.
💡

Start here: If you do nothing else, connect your website contact form to your CRM and set up one follow-up email sequence. This alone captures every lead properly and increases your conversion rate significantly — and it takes about 2 hours to set up.
